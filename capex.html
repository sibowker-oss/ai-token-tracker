The Historical Parallel

In the 1999-2001 telecom bubble, fiber optic capacity was overbuilt by ~10x relative to demand. Companies like Global Crossing, WorldCom, and 360networks spent hundreds of billions laying fiber that took 15+ years to fully utilize. Some bears argue the AI GPU buildout has similar dynamics: massive capital deployment based on demand projections that may take much longer to materialize than investors expect. The difference: AI infrastructure depreciates in 3-5 years, unlike fiber which lasted decades. If demand doesn't arrive quickly, the GPUs become worthless — unlike dark fiber that could wait.

The Bull Rebuttal

Infrastructure buildout always precedes the applications that justify it. Amazon built AWS capacity years before cloud computing became mainstream. The $250B in capex isn't all inference — a large portion is training next-generation models that may unlock new capability tiers. Revenue is growing at 3-5x annually. Anthropic went from $1B to $14B ARR in 14 months. The coding use case alone (Cursor, Copilot, Devin) is creating genuine, measurable productivity gains. And token prices are falling 10x/year — what costs $1 today will cost $0.10 next year, making AI viable for vastly more use cases.

The real question isn't whether the capex will be justified — it's when and by whom. If it takes 5 years instead of 2, many investors will lose money, but the infrastructure will eventually be used.
