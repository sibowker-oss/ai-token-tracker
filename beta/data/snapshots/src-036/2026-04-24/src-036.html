Revenue

Sacra estimates that Perplexity hit $500M in annualized revenue in April 2026, up 335% year-over-year and up from $232M in 2025, driven by the February 2026 launch of its AI agent product Computer and a new usage-based pricing layer on top of existing subscriptions.

Perplexity now serves more than 100M monthly active users from its search and agent tools, including tens of thousands of enterprise clients. Management's December 2024 roadmap still targets $656M in revenue by end-2026, implying continued compounded monthly growth from today.

Valuation & Funding

Perplexity was valued at $20B after raising $200M from investors in a round announced in September 2025.

The company had previously raised $500M in December 2024 at a $9B valuation, up from $3B in June 2024 and $520M in January 2024. Founded in 2022, Perplexity has raised approximately $1.22B in total funding.

Notable investors include Accel, IVP, SoftBank Vision Fund 2, Nvidia, and Jeff Bezos. At ~$148M in annualized revenue in June 2025, the valuation implies an approximate 120x revenue multiple.

Product

About 94% of all searches on Google today are 1-5 word queries, both basic information requests and high-intent searches like "pizza near me" against which Google has built a $260B/year business selling ads.

OpenAI's ChatGPT launched with the promise of replacing Google for more complex queries, but with training data that's still stuck in January 2022 for free accounts, it can't give up-to-date information.

Perplexity (2022) found initial product-market fit answering complex queries like "How do I provide a California employee with health insurance?" by pulling up the top search results on Bing and passing them into OpenAI's GPT-3.5 to synthesize the content into an AI-generated summary with citations.

By using GPT-3.5 and Bing, Perplexity was able to quickly launch a compelling search product without needing to build their own web crawler or search index.

Perplexity has since transitioned to open source large language models, fine-tuning versions of Mistral's 7B parameter model and Meta's LLaMa-2 to power its search summaries, and through its partnership with Microsoft, now accesses multiple frontier models including OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI systems—as well as Chinese open-source providers including DeepSeek's R1 and Moonshot's Kimi—via Microsoft's Foundry platform.

Beyond its core answer engine, Perplexity has expanded into a suite of adjacent products that extend its AI capabilities across user workflows. Perplexity Labs, available to Pro subscribers, enables users to generate reports, spreadsheets, dashboards, and simple web apps—tasks the company says previously took days can now be completed in about 10 minutes. Comet, an AI-powered browser built on Chromium, offers features like an AI sidebar assistant for summarization, workflow automation, and workspace organization, available to Pro users on Windows and macOS (launched September 2025), with Android pre-orders open and iOS support in development. Rounding out the suite is Computer, Perplexity's agentic product (launched February 2026), which carries out tasks on users' behalf—such as shopping, summarizing social media feeds, and sending emails—following voice and text commands; it introduced a usage-based credits model for top-tier subscribers and is now available to all Mac users after an initial rollout to Max subscribers.

Business Model

Perplexity operates on a freemium model, offering both a free tier for casual users and a $20/month Perplexity Pro subscription for power users.

The free tier allows users to ask a limited number of questions per month and access a subset of Perplexity's AI models. This tier serves as a customer acquisition funnel, allowing potential subscribers to experience the value of the product before committing to a paid plan.

Perplexity Pro subscribers get unlimited queries, access to Perplexity's full suite of language models (including GPT-4 and Claude), and premium features like the ability to upload files for the AI to analyze and summarize. At $20/month, the Pro plan is priced competitively with other AI subscriptions like ChatGPT Plus.

With the February 2026 launch of its Computer agent product, Perplexity added a usage-based pricing layer: top-tier subscribers receive a set number of credits for agentic tasks before paying for additional use.

This reflects the higher per-query compute costs of running autonomous agents versus standard search, and introduces a more variable revenue stream alongside the existing subscription base. Perplexity has dropped advertising as a monetization channel, citing concerns that it would hurt user trust in AI.

Beyond subscriptions, Perplexity has layered on additional monetization channels including content partnerships, distribution deals, and commerce integrations.

Getty and Perplexity signed a multi-year licensing agreement (October 2025) to display Getty's images across Perplexity's AI search and discovery tools, delivering licensed visuals via API with proper attribution, credits, and source links.

Perplexity has also pursued distribution-based monetization through OEM and platform deals. Its global Motorola partnership (April 2025) preinstalls Perplexity on millions of smartphones and bundles three months of Pro for new Razr and Edge 60 users, creating a subsidized acquisition channel at device activation.

The U.S. General Services Administration partnership (November 2025) allows federal agencies to procure Enterprise Pro for Government through MAS IT at essentially no cost for up to 18 months, establishing Perplexity as the first major AI company to enter a direct U.S. government contract and seeding a potentially large public-sector revenue stream.

Competition

The market for AI-powered search is heating up, with several key players, including tech giants like Google and Microsoft, as well as AI startups like Anthropic and OpenAI each vying to dominate the next generation of search.

OpenAI and Anthropic

ChatGPT and Perplexity are battling over who can aggregate consumer demand for AI chat—ChatGPT had a head start, but churn spiked into the 20% range last summer, while Perplexity's niche approach predicated on weekly usage has resulted in stronger retention dynamics.

That said, OpenAI is now developing its own search index and web crawler, positioning it to build its own comprehensive AI-native search engine—equipped with OpenAI's brand awareness, funding, and technical expertise behind it.

To combat this threat, Perplexity has been investing heavily in fine-tuning open source models like Meta's LLaMa to have its own proprietary model options. Their bet is that owning the search data and user relationships will prove more defensible than the language models themselves.

Anthropic, the startup behind the Claude AI assistant, is also reportedly building its own search product powered by proprietary models and search index. With over $1B in funding and a reputation for AI safety, Anthropic could prove a strong competitor, particularly for privacy-conscious users.

Big Cloud

The biggest long-term competition for Perplexity is likely to be companies like Google and Microsoft and Amazon. With far greater revenue scale and distribution, these giants have the resources to invest heavily in AI to overhaul their search products.

Google already has a chatbot, Gemini, the search data flywheel from their 8.5B searches a day and 1.5B MAUs and ownership of platforms Search, Chrome, and Android—but between their advertiser-focused incentives and big org politics, they've barely scratched the surface of building an AI-based consumer product.

Google has launched AI shopping features in the US (November 2025) that bring conversational product search, side‑by‑side comparisons, price tracking, and an agentic checkout flow into Search and the Gemini app. Google is also rolling out "Let Google Call," an AI agent that phones local stores to check stock and promotions with disclosure and opt‑out for merchants, and will complete purchases via Google Pay with participating retailers like Wayfair, Chewy, Quince, and select Shopify sellers.

Microsoft, meanwhile, has integrated OpenAI's GPT technology into its Bing search engine and is leveraging its close partnership with OpenAI to improve the product.

Amazon

Amazon filed suit against Perplexity on November 4, 2025, over the startup's agentic shopping features in the Comet browser, with a preliminary injunction hearing scheduled for February 13, 2026. Amazon argues that automated agents must identify themselves and comply with site rules, while Perplexity maintains its agent has the same permissions as a human user and has accused Amazon of trying to dictate how consumers use AI assistants online.

TAM Expansion

Perplexity's plan to build the single best user experience for search starts with capturing a slice of Google's most complex queries, then leveraging that proprietary user engagement data to build a data flywheel that continually improves their results.

While Perplexity is built on top of Google search results today, their goal is to use their own data to back into iteratively rebuilding the search stack with AI-native components, from crawler to index to relevance to rankings to personalization.

Looking forward, the key dynamics in understanding Perplexity's growth potential hinges on (1) the shift from traditional search to AI-powered search, (2) Perplexity's ability to build a differentiated search product, and (3) expansion into high-value verticals like healthcare.

Search is shifting

The rapid advancements in large language models and generative AI are fundamentally reshaping the search landscape. Users are increasingly expecting search engines to provide direct, conversational answers to their queries rather than a list of links. This shift plays to the strengths of AI-native search products like Perplexity.

Moreover, the rise of AI is expanding the total addressable market for search. While traditional search is primarily used for navigational and informational queries, AI search products are increasingly being used for complex research, analysis, and decision-making tasks. This opens up new use cases and user segments for search products.

As more users adopt AI-powered search assistants for their daily information needs, the market is poised for significant growth. If Perplexity can capture even a small percentage of this expanding market, it could translate into substantial revenue growth.

Differentiating on AI-native search

Perplexity's ability to sustain its growth trajectory will depend on its ability to build a search product that is meaningfully better than the alternatives. The company's current focus on factual accuracy, up-to-date information, and user experience provides a strong foundation.

By continuously iterating on its product based on user feedback and engagement data, Perplexity can create a virtuous cycle where its search results get better over time. As users recognize the superior quality of Perplexity's answers, it could drive increased adoption and retention.

Perplexity's data flywheel strategy—using proprietary engagement data to improve its search stack—could be a significant competitive advantage. As Perplexity builds out its own crawler, index, and ranking algorithms tailored to its users' needs, it could create a search product that is difficult for competitors to replicate.

Furthermore, by expanding into adjacent product lines like browser extensions and enterprise search, Perplexity can increase its touch points with users and capture a greater share of the overall search market. If executed well, these product expansions could significantly increase Perplexity's total addressable market.

Vertical expansion into healthcare

Healthcare is one of the largest and most query-intensive information markets, and Perplexity's AI health tool opens a direct path into it. By integrating with EHR systems and wearable data, Perplexity moves beyond general knowledge queries into a regulated vertical where accuracy and personalization command premium pricing and create durable switching costs.
