- Revenue from products that existed before AI was added (relabeled)
- Revenue from bundles where AI was given free to retain customers
- Revenue from platforms like "Data Cloud" that are data products, not AI products
- Consumption revenue from cloud infrastructure, not SaaS AI

## 3.2 Summary Verdict on AI Growth Impact

**Of the top 10 enterprise SaaS vendors:**
- **0 have seen meaningful growth ACCELERATION attributable to AI** (Microsoft's acceleration is infra, not SaaS; Shopify's is merchant/GMV growth)
- **4 have DECELERATED** despite heavy AI investment (Salesforce, Workday, Atlassian, ServiceNow slightly)
- **3 are FLAT** (Adobe, Intuit, SAP)
- **2 show acceleration from other factors** (Oracle from infra demand, Shopify from merchant growth)
- **1 maintains consistent growth** (ServiceNow -- AI helped maintain, not accelerate)

**Critical insight:** If AI were truly driving incremental demand, we would expect to see growth acceleration among vendors with significant AI SKU revenue. Instead, growth rates are flat to decelerating across the board. This strongly suggests that AI features are:
1. Replacing features that would have been built anyway
2. Being used defensively to prevent churn rather than drive expansion
3. Being bundled free to remain competitive
4. Not yet delivering enough ROI for customers to significantly expand spending

## 4.2 Penetration Reality Check

**Microsoft 365 Copilot: The Flagship Test Case**

- **450 million** commercial seats in the installed base
- **15 million** paid Copilot seats (3.3% penetration)
- After **2 full years** on market (launched Sept 2023)
- With **40-60% discounting** reported by Citi and JP Morgan
- With **70% of Fortune 500** having "adopted" it -- but only for a tiny fraction of their seats
- **70% of users who tried it preferred it initially, but only 8% kept choosing it** after trying alternatives (Recon Analytics)
- Microsoft's own paid AI subscriber share fell from 18.8% to 11.5% between July 2025 and January 2026

**GitHub Copilot: The Success Story**

- The most genuinely successful enterprise AI SKU
- 4.7M paid subscribers, 90% of Fortune 100
- Revenue exceeding what all of GitHub generated when acquired for $7.5B
- But still only ~4.7% of total GitHub users
- 42% market share of AI coding assistants, but Cursor growing fast (from $200M to $500M ARR in months)

**Salesforce Agentforce: Early but Noisy**

- 29,000 deals closed in first 15 months
- $800M ARR -- but ~$2.9B when bundled with Data Cloud and Informatica acquisition
- $800M is <2% of Salesforce's $41.5B revenue
- Consumption-based pricing at $2/conversation makes TAM calculation difficult

# KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

## Finding 1: The "AI Revenue" Inflation Factor is ~4-5x
Vendors collectively claim ~$52B in AI revenue. Real incremental SaaS AI revenue is ~$9-13B. The inflation factor is approximately **4-5x**, driven by relabeling existing products, bundling acquisitions, counting infrastructure revenue, and broad definitions.

## Finding 2: AI Has NOT Accelerated Enterprise SaaS Growth
Zero of the top 10 enterprise SaaS vendors have seen growth acceleration clearly attributable to AI products. Salesforce, Workday, and others have actually decelerated despite billions in AI investment. The most generous interpretation: AI has helped maintain existing growth rates for some vendors (ServiceNow).

## Finding 3: Real AI SKU Revenue is ~4-5% of Enterprise SaaS
Against a ~$250B enterprise SaaS market, genuine incremental AI revenue is approximately $9-13 billion or 3.5-5%. This is growing rapidly (~100%+ YoY) but from a very small base.

## Finding 4: Penetration Rates are Stubbornly Low
M365 Copilot at 3.3% after 2 years. GitHub Copilot at 4.7% after 3 years. These are the BEST cases with the largest installed bases and most distribution power in enterprise software history. Other AI SKUs have even lower penetration.

## Finding 5: The Realistic Ceiling is 6-11% by 2028
Even with aggressive growth assumptions, AI SKU revenue will likely represent only 6-11% of the enterprise SaaS market by 2027-2028. The market narrative of "AI transforming enterprise software" is accurate directionally but wildly overstated in magnitude.

## Finding 6: AI is a Margin Story, Not a Revenue Story (For Most)
For most enterprise SaaS vendors, AI's real value is:
- Internal cost reduction (Intuit saved $90M in efficiencies)
- Churn prevention (Adobe: Firefly subscribers have 22% higher LTV)
- Upselling to premium tiers (Atlassian: Premium/Enterprise +40%)
- NOT standalone incremental revenue at scale
